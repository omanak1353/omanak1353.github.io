Omana “Meena” Kariampuzha passed away on January 20, 2022, at the age of 79, in her home in Austin, TX, surrounded by her family. 

She was born in Chempu, Kerala, India, to the late Varghese and Maria Kandathil. She graduated with a botany degree from St. Joseph’s College in Alleppey. After marriage, she moved to Mombasa, Kenya, where she taught biology at the Star of the Sea Secondary School and Khamis Secondary School for 25 years. Later, when Meena and her family moved to Austin, Texas, she was a homemaker and a substitute teacher. She had a passion for cooking and gardening, and enjoyed painting, writing poetry, reading the Bible and learning Spanish. Meena’s kindness extended to anyone who crossed her path. She loved to entertain guests in her home but her greatest joy was cooking for her family and spending time with them.

She is survived by her husband of 58 years, William Kariampuzha, her children George Kariampuzha (Sylvia), Charles Kariampuzha (Lekha), Joseph Kariampuzha (Irene), and Rosemaria Kariampuzha Pallan (Joseph), and her grandchildren Kelli Kariampuzha, Sharon Kariampuzha, William Z. Kariampuzha, Karena Kariampuzha, Thomas Pallan, Maria Kariampuzha, William Pallan, William M. Kariampuzha, Serena Pallan and Annrose Kariampuzha. In addition, she is survived by her siblings Thomas Kandathil, Elizabeth Johnnie (Johnnie), Susan Chembakassery (George), and numerous cousins, nieces and nephews.

A memorial mass will be held 2 p.m. on March 5, 2022 at Emmaus Catholic Church, Lakeway, Texas.

In lieu of flowers, donations can be made in Meena’s honor to CurePSP. Progressive supranuclear palsy (PSP) is a neurodegenerative disease that has no known cause or cure, which affects brain cells that control balance, coordination, eye movement and speech.
